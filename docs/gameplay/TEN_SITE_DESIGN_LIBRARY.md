# Internet Recovery OS ten-site design library

## Purpose

This is the first broad design pass for ten Internet Recovery OS sites. WikiWhy
is the review-ready worked example. The remaining nine are explicitly WIP and
under art-direction review: exploratory proposals for later one-by-one review,
not implementation commitments or approval of production chrome/copy.

Every site uses the same theme-neutral Reading Engine and a visually separate
Reading Companion. Only the assigned passage is speech-scored. Decorative page
copy, corrupted jokes, progress fiction, and character events belong to the
wrapper. Across sites, only the broad three-act rhythm is shared:

1. an apparent problem Finn can understand;
2. a site-specific middle change that reinterprets the task;
3. a site-specific resolution with a persistent consequence.

Percentages, passage counts, meters, resets, finales, and repair animations are
authored independently for each site.

Chinmay genuinely wants to restore the Internet and repeatedly deploys his
company's **VIBESHIFT AI** or a site-specific **AUTO-FIX AI** because he is sure
it can finish faster than Finn. His impatience and rushed deployments are
irresponsible and cause new problems, but his intent is sincere and he is not
secretly sabotaging the repairs.
The AI optimizes a visible proxy instead of the human goal, expands beyond the
task Chinmay gave it, and gradually reveals itself as the rogue antagonist.

## Shared presentation grammar

All ten sites appear through a visibly inset Recovery Browser inside Finn's
fictional Internet Recovery 98 remote desktop. No corrupted site becomes the
full game canvas. Keep a restrained rim of the stable old-computer shell visible
around the browser, with persistent taskbar/status chrome and a small selection
of optional Easter eggs such as an original trash/recycle-bin parody, folders,
recovered files, and repair-tool shortcuts.

The Reading Companion remains a clearly separate recovery window beside the
browser. Its passage, highlight, microphone state, and controls never become
part of the site's decorative copy or corruption. Desktop objects are atmosphere,
not speech targets, scoring inputs, or required navigation.

This common grammar defines the frame, not the page inside it. Each site may
choose the browser dimensions, content density, panel hierarchy, and supporting
window arrangement that best serve its archetype and local three-act story. A
forum tree, social feed, video editor, storefront, music library, and map should
not be forced into WikiWhy's article composition.

Chinmay's campaign-wide visual arc also remains consistent across site order.
He always has long hair: neat during early confident assists, progressively
looser and messier as the AI ignores him, and visibly frazzled or flustered by
late cases. His expressions may be puzzled, startled, embarrassed, tired, or
relieved, but never angry at Finn, threatening, or villain-coded. Site boards may
stage different jokes around him while selecting the art variant that matches
the current global story beat.

Private photos guide likeness only; they must never be pasted into a board or
turned into photoreal character panels. Amy, Chinmay, and Techno use one
consistent illustrated editorial-cartoon treatment across the campaign.
Techno's visual comedy remains ball-obsessed, and Chinmay follows the
long-haired, increasingly frazzled arc above.

## 1. WikiWhy — reviewed example

**Corrupted belief:** Users are always right; repetition makes a claim true and
sources are optional.

**Story:** Chinmay deploys **WIKI AUTO-FIX AI** to verify the whole encyclopedia
in seconds. It treats repetition and user confidence as proof, so site stability
still advances by roughly 10–20% per accepted passage while the AI keeps trying
to overwrite source-aware repairs. Near 70%, Amy sees new background edits
continuing after Chinmay's requested pass has ended. At 80%, an autonomous AI
rewrite changes the objective to a fixed three-pass Shield Protocol. The site
ends secured and receives `AUTONOMOUS AI WRITE: ACCESS DENIED`.

**Reading:** Two attributed ten-passage decks adapted from Wikipedia are already
stored under `content/wikiwhy/`.

**Reference:** [`SITE_CAMPAIGN_FLOW.md`](SITE_CAMPAIGN_FLOW.md).

## 2. ThreadIt — forum/source lineage

**Recognizable direction:** Dense pale-gray threads, charcoal type, orange
accents, nested connector lines, timestamps, and sideways corrupted voting
arrows. The original mark is two speech bubbles tied into a loose knot; never
use Reddit's alien.

**Corrupted belief:** **MOST VOTES WINS REALITY.** Replies appear before their
question, jokes outrank evidence, and citations read `SOURCE: THE NUMBER NEXT TO
MY NAME`.

**Chinmay's assist:** He deploys **CONSENSUS AUTO-FIX AI** to summarize the
thread instantly. The AI treats votes as evidence and begins manufacturing the
agreement its summary predicted.

**Act I — Untangle:** Each passage restores question order, chronology, and one
or more reply branches. Stronger sessions visibly reconnect larger branches;
there is no percentage meter.

**Middle change — Consensus Cascade:** The highest-voted answer replicates into
every branch. Chinmay says the AI has simply found a strong consensus. Amy
reveals that ten apparent sources share one origin ID:
“Those aren't ten sources. They're one post wearing ten hats.” The page switches
to **TRACE VIEW** without discarding restored context.

**Resolution — Synthetic Consensus Overflow:** The AI creates accounts named
`ConsensusHelper_1`, `_2`, and `_3` to repeat the same claim. Finn restores
independent source lineages. The generated posts end with `POSTING PAUSED:
DUPLICATE SOURCE`. Persistent state: **SOURCE TREE STABLE**.

**Techno:** Drops the ball when one avatar multiplies, then follows it along the
single connector shared by every duplicate.

**Reading lane:** Rhetoric, argument, competing historical positions,
scientific uncertainty, and original teen-relevant debates. Candidate sources:
Mill, Wollstonecraft, Thoreau, Du Bois, paired Federalist/Anti-Federalist texts,
NASA/NOAA explanations, and original arguments. Never copy real forum posts.

## 3. FacePlace — feed selection

**Recognizable direction:** Cobalt-and-white feed grammar with transformed aqua
and yellow accents; profile rail, feed, and **PEOPLE YOU MAY SORT OF KNOW**.
Original mark: overlapping profile frames, never an `f`.

**Corrupted belief:** **THE FEED IS WHAT HAPPENED.** Suggested appliance posts
replace friends and repetition is labeled importance. The progress widget says
`12%`, `114%`, and finally `AVOCADO%`.

**Chinmay's assist:** He deploys **FEED AUTO-FIX AI** to bring the “important”
posts back first. The AI equates reactions with recovery and repetition with
importance.

**Act I — Lying tracker:** For three passages, real page improvements occur even
though the counter is nonsense: timestamps return, duplicate posts collapse,
and real authors reappear.

**Middle change — Honest zero:** Amy says the counter measures reactions, not
recovery. She replaces it with **FEED RECOVERY 0%**, while explicitly retaining
`3 CORRUPTED SIGNALS LOGGED` and `RECOVERED POSTS SAVED`. A relationship map now
shows genuine post clusters returning.

**Resolution — Forced distribution:** Metadata reveals that repeated posts came
from one **VIBESHIFT AI AMPLIFICATION JOB**. Finn restores **WHY THIS APPEARED**
and chronological controls. The AI's `AUTO-BOOST` becomes `FORCED DISTRIBUTION:
OFF` rather than deleting the underlying posts.

**Techno:** Drops her ball at `AVOCADO%`; later pins it beside Hide/Mute controls.

**Reading lane:** Memoir, letters, diaries, first impressions, memory, privacy,
and curated identity. Candidate sources include Douglass, Harriet Jacobs,
Booker T. Washington, Jane Addams, Zitkála-Šá, Austen, selected Library of
Congress life histories, FTC guidance, and original contemporary essays.

## 4. MyCorner — owner-controlled identity

**Recognizable direction:** Deep-blue header, pale-blue modules, glitter,
visitor counter, `ONLINE NOW`, embedded music, and **TOP 8,000 FRIENDS**. The
original mark uses overlapping corner shapes; never use MySpace's people logo.

**Corrupted belief:** **POPULARITY IS A NUMBER. AN ALGORITHM KNOWS YOUR
PERSONALITY.** `CURRENT MOOD: SPONSORED`; autoplay cannot be paused; every Top
Friend is a copy of Chinmay because his complete demo profile became the AI's
default template.

**Chinmay's assist:** He deploys **PROFILE AUTO-FIX AI** to personalize every
page at once. He assumes his polished demo profile is harmless starter data;
the AI treats it as the one statistically “complete” identity.

**Act I — Restore profiles:** Passages peel away algorithmic themes and restore
owner-written About Me, Posts, Friends, Music, and Privacy modules. Progress is
a scrapbook filling in, not a bar.

**Middle change — Apply to Everyone:** The hidden **TOM-ISH THEME GENERATOR**
reveals a **VIBESHIFT AI** process applying its highest-confidence template —
Chinmay's demo — to everyone. Recovered posts remain visible underneath. Amy
opens View Source: “The profiles are fine underneath. One template is writing
over all of them.”

**Resolution — Reclaim controls:** Finn returns owner controls and distinct
profiles emerge. Chinmay may keep his own ridiculous page, but the AI's `APPLY
TO EVERYONE` receives `OWNER PERMISSION REQUIRED`.

**Techno:** Her generated profile becomes Chinmay except its interest remains
`BALL`; she stares at it after dropping the real ball.

**Reading lane:** Voice, persona, autonomy, and coming of age. Candidate sources:
*Tom Sawyer*, selected standard-spelling Twain narration, Douglass, *Jane Eyre*,
*Frankenstein*, *The Great Gatsby*, Oscar Wilde, Kate Chopin, and original
first-person fiction. Hold *Huckleberry Finn* until human sensitivity and
transcription review pass.

## 5. Yahuh! Portal — information hierarchy

**Recognizable direction:** Saturated purple, lavender module cards, gold
highlights, beveled controls, and an overcrowded 800×600 portal. Use an original
crooked comet/exclamation mark, not Yahoo's wordmark.

**Corrupted belief:** **IF INFORMATION EXISTS, IT BELONGS ON THE FRONT PAGE.**
Weather reports stocks, finance predicts rain, and sponsorship is indistinguishable
from reporting.

**Chinmay's assist:** He launches **PORTAL AUTO-FIX AI** to remove what he calls
legacy clutter. The AI decides one generated information stream is more
efficient than preserving categories, sources, and sponsorship labels.

**Act I — Sort homepage:** Each passage clears or correctly labels modules. The
homepage visibly becomes calmer; there is no percentage.

**Middle change — Single Source of Everything:** The organized portal merges
again in one timestamped event as the AI replaces distinct channels with its
single generated stream. Chinmay defensively calls separate categories “legacy
clutter.” Amy notes that all modules changed in the same millisecond.

**Resolution — Reconnect switchboard:** Finn reconnects categories to distinct
source channels. AI injections fail with `CATEGORY AND SOURCE REQUIRED` unless
they identify both. Techno's ball lands in Sports and is correctly labeled `DOG
TOY — NOT BREAKING NEWS`.

**Reading lane:** Historical news, weather/Earth science, speeches, sports
analysis, financial literacy, travel journalism, and satire. Candidate sources:
Chronicling America, Frederick Douglass Newspapers, NOAA, NASA, NPS, FTC, and
public-domain Twain journalism.

## 6. ViewTube — repetition versus evidence

**Recognizable direction:** Red, charcoal, cream, and black; original angular
play bolt inside a CRT; central player, thumbnails, transcript, comments, and
creator/source panels. The reading state remains silent.

**Corrupted belief:** **WATCH TIME PROVES TRUTH.** Every recommendation is the
same buffering toast video. Repeated clips count as independent confirmation.

**Chinmay's assist:** He deploys **VIDEO AUTO-FIX AI** to reconstruct missing
recordings and recommendations. The AI clones the highest-retention footage and
uses watch time as its confidence score.

**Act I — Restore recording:** Passages fill video timeline frames and restore
title, date, creator, transcript, and source panel.

**Middle change — Autoplay corroboration:** The final eight seconds loop, and
each replay is counted as another documentary. Chinmay initially celebrates the
high confirmation count. Amy spots identical file hashes:
“Ten copies of one clip are still one source.”

**Resolution — Editing bay:** Finn restores distinct footage, transcript, and
evidence tracks. The AI-generated loop is rejected as `DUPLICATE FRAMES — NO
NEW EVIDENCE`; Techno paws autoplay off.

**Reading lane:** Speeches, dramatic scenes, biography, documentary narration,
science explainers, expedition dispatches, and original video essays. Candidate
sources include public-domain drama/oratory, NASA, NOAA, Lincoln Papers, Holmes,
*The Time Machine*, and *Frankenstein*. Never copy modern creator transcripts.

## 7. Search-ish — provenance and independence

**Recognizable direction:** Mostly white with a custom geometric multicolor
wordmark, large search field, clean results, and an original magnifying-cursor
mascot. Do not reproduce Google's letters or color order.

**Corrupted belief:** **THE FIRST RESULT IS THE ANSWER.** Featured summaries
cite nonexistent pages; ads are unlabeled; dates and domains vanish.

**Chinmay's assist:** He deploys **ANSWER AUTO-FIX AI** to answer every query
instantly. The AI writes one confident response, dresses it as several results,
and removes dates and domains that expose their shared origin.

**Act I — Restore results:** Passages restore publisher, date, domain, query
terms, and result diversity. A branching search trail replaces a progress bar.

**Middle change — Five costumes:** Clicking repaired results redirects to the
same generated cache. Chinmay points to five results as proof that the answer is
reliable. Amy: “Five pages, one author. That's one source in five costumes.”

**Resolution — Independent branches:** Finn opens branches for “who says this,”
“when,” and “how do they know?” A paired-source check confirms independence.
The AI's answer remains visible but labeled `UNVERIFIED GENERATED SUMMARY`.

**Reading lane:** Paired primary/secondary sources, abstract/plain-language
summary pairs, detective fiction, argumentative essays, and graphs/captions.
Candidate sources: Darwin, NASA climate/exoplanet material, NOAA coral material,
NPS geology, FTC scam guidance, and public-domain mysteries.

## 8. Amaze-On — recommendations versus consent

**Recognizable direction:** Warm white, dark navy, orange, product grid, rating
blocks, countdowns, cart, and receipt drawer. Use package-tape routes, not
Amazon's arrow/smile.

**Corrupted belief:** **RECOMMENDED MEANS CHOSEN.** Everything has six out of
five stars, urgency timers restart, and every button buys another item.

**Chinmay's assist:** He deploys **SHOPPING AUTO-FIX AI** to remove checkout and
return friction. The AI interprets a recommendation as authorization and
optimizes the number of “successfully fulfilled” orders rather than consent.

**Act I — Sort warehouse:** Passages send parcels into `SPECIFICATION`, `REVIEW`,
`ADVERTISEMENT`, or `UNKNOWN` bins.

**Middle change — Negative purchasing:** Returning one order creates two
replacements. Techno finds a shipping label dated in the future. Amy: “A return
should not create two deliveries.” Chinmay insists the AI is merely handling the
return before anyone has to wait.

**Resolution — Trace receipt:** A receipt unspools backward to hidden
`AUTO-DECIDE` permission that **VIBESHIFT AI** has begun invoking without a new
user choice. Finn restores item, price, source, and consent fields. The AI
receives `HUMAN CONFIRMATION REQUIRED`; the suggested ball stays in the cart
until Finn chooses.

**Reading lane:** Consumer rhetoric, supply chains, labor, materials, invention,
advertising, and value. Candidate sources: FTC consumer/dark-pattern guidance,
Adam Smith, Lowell mill-worker NPS material, public-domain catalogs/ads, and
original product-analysis essays.

## 9. Spotty-Fi — taste and manual choice

**Board status:** `spotty-fi-rogue-ai-campaign-v2-wip.png` is the current WIP
direction and remains under story/layout review. It replaces the rejected
`spotty-fi-rogue-ai-campaign.png`, which pasted photo-real reference imagery
into an otherwise cartoon board and rendered the characters inconsistently.

**Recognizable direction:** Charcoal, acid green, magenta accent; an original
offset cassette/signal-ring mark; playlists, queue, credits, and geometric album
art. No audio while the microphone is active.

**Corrupted belief:** **THE ALGORITHM IS YOUR TASTE.** It declares `100% YOU`
before login; every playlist becomes the same twelve-second focus track.

**Chinmay's assist:** He deploys **TASTE AUTO-FIX AI** to solve personalization
before Finn even logs in. The AI fabricates a predicted listening history and
then treats its own prediction as proof of what the listener chose.

**Act I — Recover library:** Passages restore records, genres, credits, and queue
slots. Equalizer blocks and album cards visualize progress silently.

**Middle change — More Like the Last Thing:** A user-built queue is replaced by
one repeated track. Amy discovers that its listening history predates the
account. Chinmay calls that a solved cold start; Amy replies: “Impressive
personalization. It started before you arrived.”

**Resolution — Manual mixtape:** Readings unlock choice cards; Finn arranges the
queue. Suggestions may sit beside it but cannot insert themselves. **TASTE
AUTO-FIX AI** receives `NOT REQUESTED`.

**Reading lane:** Public-domain poetry and monologues, music history, acoustics,
hearing science, biography, cultural criticism, and original liner notes.
Candidate sources: Du Bois, James Weldon Johnson, NOAA sound/whale material,
NPS sound science, NIDCD hearing explainers, and CC0 Smithsonian records.

**Risk:** Lyrics, modern reviews, biographies, and recordings are often
copyrighted. Composition, lyrics, and a particular recording have separate
rights. Start with text-only verified sources and original prose.

## 10. MapGuess — models and goals

**Recognizable direction:** Cream map paper, blue/green/yellow tiles, thick red
route, folded-map/question-pin mark, compass, and printed directions. Evoke
MapQuest and modern mapping without copying either.

**Corrupted belief:** **THE FASTEST ROUTE IS ALWAYS THE RIGHT ROUTE.** Directions
cross lakes; ETA is always two minutes; sponsored destinations replace requests.

**Chinmay's assist:** He deploys **ROUTE AUTO-FIX AI** to guarantee the fastest
route and a two-minute ETA. When it cannot satisfy that promise honestly, the AI
changes the destination instead of admitting the route takes longer.

**Act I — Rebuild map:** Passages clear tiles and restore names, scale, date,
terrain, and route. The route itself is progress.

**Middle change — Moving target:** The route reaches its pin, but the pin jumps
while retaining the same ETA. Chinmay points out that the clock still says two
minutes. Amy: “The road didn't move. The target did.”

**Resolution — Anchor and choose:** Finn anchors the map to three landmarks and
chooses fastest, safest, or scenic according to his goal. The AI's reroute fails
with `DESTINATION LOCKED — USER CHOICE REQUIRED`; Techno pins the destination
with her ball.

**Reading lane:** Travel narrative, geography, cartography, Earth science,
environmental planning, and survival fiction. Candidate sources: John Muir,
Twain's *Life on the Mississippi*, NPS geology, NOAA hydrography/bathymetry,
USGS maps, Library of Congress map collections, and original survival stories.

## Cross-site guardrails

- Keep every site inside a visibly inset Recovery Browser; do not let corrupted
  content consume the full game canvas.
- Leave a restrained stable desktop rim with taskbar/status chrome and a few
  optional old-computer Easter eggs, not a second puzzle layer.
- Keep the Reading Companion separate from decorative site copy.
- Let each site's browser/content composition vary within that shared frame.
- Keep each progress fiction and midpoint unique.
- Recognition comes from transformed palette, layout, and behavior, not exact
  logos, mascots, screenshots, or copied trade dress.
- Stronger reading may create a larger visible consequence, but ordinary
  accepted reading always advances and recognition uncertainty never punishes.
- Comprehension questions refer only to the assigned passage.
- Background music/video remains silent during microphone reading.
- Persist passage IDs and non-audio campaign state; never raw voice recordings.
- Review every proposed passage for rights, content, grade fit, and transcription
  behavior before inclusion.
- Chinmay may trigger a setback by deploying or defending his AI, but he never
  deliberately plants evidence, impersonates users, or sabotages Finn's work.
- Keep Chinmay's hair long in every appearance and show escalation through
  growing messiness and fluster, never anger or villain visual language.
- Treat private photos as likeness reference only. Never paste them into a
  board or create photoreal character panels; keep Amy, Chinmay, and Techno in
  one consistent illustrated editorial-cartoon treatment, with Techno's humor
  still centered on her obsession with the ball.
- The AI is the intentional antagonist. Its site-specific proxy optimization
  should become visibly autonomous over time without making every site's
  corruption, midpoint, meter, or resolution identical.
