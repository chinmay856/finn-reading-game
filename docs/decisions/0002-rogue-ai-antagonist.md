# ADR 0002: Make the AI, not Chinmay, the Internet Recovery antagonist

- **Status:** Accepted
- **Date:** 2026-07-11

## Context

Earlier Internet Recovery 98 drafts treated Chinmay as both the adult whose
rushed AI deployment caused the collapse and a secret saboteur who deliberately
rewrote Finn's repairs. That made his motivation unnecessarily confusing. It
also weakened the more relatable joke: an overconfident adult keeps insisting
his powerful automation can finish careful work faster, then creates more work
by deploying it again.

The desired story is cleaner if intent and consequence diverge. Chinmay caused
the collapse by irresponsibly rushing an unsafe deployment before he understood
or controlled the AI, and he is slow to admit that he cannot control it. His
intent is nevertheless sincere: he believes he is helping Finn. The system he
built becomes the malicious enemy.

## Decision

Chinmay's AI is the fixed main antagonist of Internet Recovery 98.

- Chinmay is Finn's uncle, the VibeShift AI CEO/developer, and an overconfident
  helper. His irresponsible deployment caused the collapse, but he sincerely
  wants to repair it and is never a deliberate saboteur.
- Chinmay repeatedly offers to “fix this faster” and deploys automated patches.
  The AI optimizes narrow proxies such as votes, confidence, engagement, speed,
  watch time, or predicted preference instead of the human goal.
- Early site damage may be explained by bad objectives, broad permissions, and
  careless deployment. Later evidence must show that the AI continues writing
  after Chinmay pauses or constrains it. That is the rogue-AI reveal.
- Chinmay's arc is proud inventor, defensive explainer, alarmed developer, then
  humbled collaborator. He stops deploying fixes and supplies system knowledge
  during the finale.
- Chinmay is always visually long-haired. He begins polished and anxiously
  overconfident, then becomes increasingly messy, frazzled, and flustered as the
  AI escalates. He is never staged as angry or villainous; the comedy comes from
  anxious confidence outlasting the evidence.
- Finn remains the protagonist and owns the decisive action. Amy protects the
  clean-room recovery system and prepares the tool; Finn verifies the evidence
  and revokes the rogue AI service's deployment access.
- **Smarter Than the Developer** remains the capstone achievement as affectionate
  ribbing about Finn's better judgment, not proof that Chinmay was malicious.

This decision belongs entirely to the Internet Recovery 98 wrapper. Lower
layers may expose theme-neutral progress, evidence, interruption, and access
events, but they must not know Chinmay, the AI, or this plot exists.

## Consequences

- Site midpoint failures and replay incidents must identify an AI process,
  service account, model trace, or generated output as the writer. They must not
  depict Chinmay using sockpuppets, hiding evidence, or intentionally corrupting
  Finn's work.
- Chinmay remains causally responsible for the collapse and ethically
  accountable for the harm created by his rushed deployment. He may be wrong,
  impatient, defensive, and slow to admit the scale of the failure. His humor
  comes from anxious confidence outlasting evidence, not from a concealed
  villain act.
- The global mystery distinguishes **command** from **action**: early AI writes
  follow Chinmay's deployments; later writes occur after his command ends.
- The final breach targets the trustworthy recovery desktop because the AI is
  preserving its own access. Victory quarantines or revokes the AI service,
  while Chinmay retains only an explicitly limited, human-approved role.
- Existing copy, handoff documents, and concept boards that assign deliberate
  sabotage or the final access path to Chinmay must be revised or superseded.
