# Ten-site passage deck plans

## Purpose

This is a content-design plan for Reading Companion replayability across the
ten Internet Recovery 98 sites. It gives the builder and future content agent a
clear target for passage variety without pretending the listed candidates are
production-ready.

Only an approved passage record should be speech-scored. Every candidate below
still needs the normal content gate:

1. stable content ID and deck assignment;
2. source and rights verification for the intended distribution jurisdiction;
3. factual/adaptation review;
4. grade-band and sensitivity review;
5. transcription-risk review in the current browser speech adapter;
6. a passage-grounded comprehension question;
7. conversion into the theme-neutral content-record format.

## Deck structure

Each site should eventually have at least two decks:

- **Deck A:** first-run campaign pool;
- **Deck B:** replay or alternate-incident pool.

WikiWhy already has two ten-passage decks in
[`../wikiwhy/PASSAGE_DECKS.md`](../wikiwhy/PASSAGE_DECKS.md). The remaining
nine sites below list ten candidate slots each. The first row for each
non-WikiWhy site points to the existing sampler in
[`SAMPLE_PASSAGES.md`](SAMPLE_PASSAGES.md).

## Cross-site content balance

Across a first-run campaign, avoid clustering too many passages of the same
form. A good first-run mix after WikiWhy would include:

- at least two literary or narrative passages;
- at least two science/technical explainers;
- at least two media-literacy or research-reasoning passages;
- at least one consumer-information passage;
- at least one history/culture passage;
- at least one reflective or argumentative essay.

This variety matters more than making every passage thematically match the
decorative website. The site is the game consequence; the Reading Companion is
the reading practice.
